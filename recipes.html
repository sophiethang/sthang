Macarons

What you will need:
- 1 cup of icing sugar
- 1 cup of grounded almonds
- 2 egg whites
- A pinch of salt
- 1/4 cup of caster sugar

For the filling:
- 1 and 1/2 cup of softened, unsalted butter
- 2/3 cup of icing sugar

Method:
Before you start preheat the oven to 140 degrees (fan-forced)

1. Sift the icing sugar and the grounded almonds into a large bowl, removing any lumps and mix together.
2. In another bowl, whisk the egg whites and salt together until fluffy then add a little caster sugar at a time and continue to whisk until thick and glossy
3. Gently stir in the icing sugar and grounded almonds
4. Using a piping bag, fill it with the macaron mixture. Place a paper template onto a baking sheet and pipe small blobs, remember to not pipe too much.
5. Gently tap the baking sheet a few times to help the mixure settle and leave to dry for 20 minutes.
6. Place the macarons in the oven for 7-8 minutes, open the oven to release any steam and cook for another 7-8 minutes.
7. Transfer onto cooling rack and leave to cool, do not remove until they are cold.

Making the Filling:

1. Beat the softened butter until fluffy, gradually beating in the icing sugar.

2. Place 1/2 a teaspoon of the filling onto the flat side of the macaron and sandwich together with a very small twist.

Refrigerate for 24 hours for a chewier, tastier texture.
